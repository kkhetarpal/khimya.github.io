Khimya Khetarpal

I am a Ph.D. candidate with Prof. Doina Precup in the Reasoning and Learning Lab at McGill University and Mila, Montreal.

Prior to this, I was working in the Perceptual Computing space on Object Recognition at Intel Corporation, with application to self-driving cars. I completed my Masters in the Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Florida, Gainesville. At UF, I was fortunate to work under the supervision of Prof. Eakta Jain, on modeling saliency in comic art and Dr. Eric M. Schwartz, in the Machine Intelligence Laboratory. My research interests span reinforcement learning, computer vision, and robotics.

I worked as a Research Associate, in the Intelligent Systems Laboratory at Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur advised by Prof. Laxmidhar Behera. Before joining IITK, I was working at Robert Bosch as a Software Engineer. I completed my undergraduate studies at VIT University, India.

Research

My work focuses on learning generalized temporal abstractions across both action and perception in the context of continual reinforcement learning also known as never-ending learning or lifelong learning. Just like humans acquire skills and build on top of already existing skills to solve more complicated tasks, I aim to build AI agents that learn and develop skills continually, hierarchically and incrementally over time.
